# Management & Leadership in CS Final

## Written

### Initiative

#### Scope
My initiative was part of the collaboration group. We were introduced to the issue of improving interaction between different research teams. One of the main challenges identified was that when a team encounters an issue, another team may have already experienced and resolved that same issue. Therefore, my initiative focused on developing a framework for coworking office hours so that researchers from different groups could organically interact, collaborate, and support one another.

The scope of this initiative involved writing an SOP document outlining how anyone within HAAG can set up their own coworking session. Because this research is conducted online, it can often feel isolating, especially when research groups are not very active. This initiative provides an opportunity for individuals to interact and network with members of other teams.

#### Evidence
I had conversations with other managers who shared similar feelings of isolation within their groups.

In my personal experience, I have participated in online coworking sessions, and they have been highly effective.

Research on social facilitation and virtual accountability suggests that online coworking can improve focus, productivity, and task engagement by providing social presence and external structure in remote work environments. Studies on “body doubling” and digitally mediated collaboration further indicate that working alongside others virtually may help reduce procrastination and support sustained attention.

#### Procedures
The primary procedure I developed was an SOP document outlining how anyone can set up a coworking session in a relatively simple and low-effort manner. The purpose of this procedure is to encourage individuals within HAAG to organize and participate in their own coworking sessions.
