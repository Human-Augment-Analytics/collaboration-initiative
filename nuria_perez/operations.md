## How are you assessing your role as the project manager?

I have found that I am most effective in my role when I am able to consistently attend the team’s biweekly connect meetings. During those times, I have better visibility into progress, team dynamics, and potential blockers.

However, I have not been able to attend as consistently as I would like, which has made it more difficult to stay fully aligned with the team. This has impacted my ability to proactively track progress and support the group in real time.

---

## What challenges & success have you observed in your group?

Challenges:
- Inconsistent visibility into team progress due to limited attendance in meetings  
- Limited interaction with the computational advisor, which makes it harder to fully understand team progress and direction  
- Some inconsistency in weekly report submissions for certain team members 
- A process gap around repository migration: the idea of moving the project repo into the HAAG organization was discussed via Slack and received positive feedback, but no follow-up action has been taken yet. This has made it unclear how to proceed or who owns the next steps.

Successes:
- Strong individual contributions when tasks are clearly defined  
- Overall team is making progress toward project goals  

---

## How are you collaborating with stakeholders to ensure your role is supporting and motivating the group?

I have primarily collaborated through Slack communication and by monitoring weekly reports. However, I recognize that limited direct interaction with the computational advisor and reduced meeting attendance has made collaboration less effective than it could be.

Moving forward, I plan to be more consistent in attending meetings and engaging directly with both the team and the computational advisor to better support progress and address challenges earlier.

---

## What feedback have you received on your role (solicited or unsolicited)?

Feedback has mostly been indirect through team responsiveness and participation. There has not been significant direct feedback, which suggests that communication is functioning, but there is still room to be more proactive in engagement.

- Improve consistency in attending biweekly meetings
