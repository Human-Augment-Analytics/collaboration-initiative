# HAAG Collaboration Initiative

---

## Scope of Initiative

This initiative establishes a standardized collaboration framework for teams operating within HAAG, regardless of how those teams were formed, whether pre-existing groups from other courses, newly assembled HAAG teams, or research groups joining collectively. The scope focuses specifically on interaction patterns: how team members communicate, stay visible to one another, and engage with each other's work on a weekly basis. It does not govern project milestones or technical deliverables, but rather the structural habits that make project work transparent and accountable.

The procedure is designed to be adaptive across HAAG's diverse team compositions, including applied research settings such as the UF BioVision Lab. It introduces three blendable collaboration practices (High Dialogue, Peer Visibility, and Synchronization) alongside a Slack communication standard and a mandatory weekly peer report review process. Together, these mechanisms ensure that no sub-team operates in isolation and that stuck points surface early rather than at final deadlines.

---

## Evidence of Alignment with HAAG's Goals

Evidence of alignment came from both direct observation and documented communication. The problem this procedure addresses, specifically a lack of structured visibility and peer engagement across research teams, was something I observed firsthand while working with the 3D Fossils team. Weekly reports were already a HAAG requirement, referenced in the course syllabus, but the expectation around how they were submitted, reviewed, and integrated into team meetings was not clearly enforced in practice.

When the procedure was introduced, some team members initially pushed back, stating that the requirements felt redundant. Dr. Wolcott reached out to confirm whether her current method was sufficient. After consulting directly with the HAAG team to clarify what would meet the standard, I communicated back to everyone that the procedure was not optional and that compliance was tied to their grade per the course syllabus. That context resolved the resistance and there were no further issues. These exchanges took place across both public and private Slack channels, and the syllabus itself serves as a supporting reference. Lack of structured collaboration visibility was not the only challenge present, but it was one that directly aligned with HAAG's goals around transparency and accountability.

## Procedures Generated

**Procedure:** HAAG Collaboration Procedure

**Purpose:** To formalize how HAAG team members interact, not just what they produce. The procedure ensures team-wide visibility, early identification of blockers, and consistent peer engagement through structured weekly report review, adaptive collaboration intensity levels, and a Slack communication standard. It also defines clear enforcement responsibilities for the Project Manager, anchored to existing HAAG deadlines and grading structures.
