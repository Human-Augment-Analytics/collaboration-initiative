# Collaboration Initiative – Visible Collective Methodology Space

## Problem
Currently in HAAG and project teams, work and knowledge are scattered. People don’t have clear visibility into what others are doing, and useful strategies (e.g., how someone approaches a task, tools they use, lessons learned) are rarely shared across the team.

## Preliminary Research Question
How can we create a visible collaboration space that helps team members not only share work, but also exchange strategies and learn from each other in remote research teams?

## Proposed Solution
Create a shared space where people can:
- show what they are working on  
- share how they are doing it (methods, tools, strategies)  
- learn from others’ approaches  

The goal is not just coordination, but making thinking and process more visible.

## Audience
HAAG, CS 8803 ORO managers, Training Batch Selection team (led by Dr. Mussman)

## Technology
TBD (maybe GitHub, Slack, etc.)

## Idea
1. Create a lightweight “strategy + work sharing” space:
   - weekly post: what I did + how I approached it  
   - quick strategy drops (tips, tools, workflows)  
2. Encourage cross-team visibility (not just within subgroups)  
3. Keep it informal and low-effort to encourage participation  
4. Highlight useful strategies to reinforce sharing behavior  

## Validation
- Surveys/interviews on:
  - awareness of others’ work  
  - usefulness of shared strategies  
  - perceived learning from teammates  

- Compare before vs after:
  - cross-team awareness  
  - reuse of strategies/tools  

- Track participation (posts, interactions)

## Plan
- Week 1: observe current sharing patterns  
- Week 2: introduce structure + examples  
- Week 3–4: pilot with different teams (HAAG, managers, training batch project)  
- Ongoing: iterate based on participation and feedback  

## Risks / Blockers
- Low participation (people don’t share)  
- Posts become too long or too vague  
- Hard to maintain consistency over time
