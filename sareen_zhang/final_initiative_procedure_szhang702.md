# Visible Collective Methodology Space  
**Sareen Zhang**

---

## 1. Initiative Scope

My initiative focuses on creating a visible collaboration space where both work and process are shared across teams in HAAG. Currently, most communication happens through weekly reports and meetings, which primarily capture what people did, but not how they approached problems. As a result, useful strategies, workflows, and lessons learned are rarely shared, and knowledge remains scattered across individuals and channels.  

To address this, I designed a lightweight, low-pressure sharing system that complements existing workflows. The idea is to introduce a separate space (e.g., a Slack channel) where each post captures a small, high-value takeaway—either an insight (tool, workflow, strategy) or a blocker (question or issue). The goal is not to replace existing updates, but to make thinking and process visible, enabling team members to learn from each other and reuse strategies more naturally.  

---

## 2. Alignment with HAAG Goals

This initiative aligns with HAAG’s goals of supporting collaboration, knowledge sharing, and research efficiency.  

From observations within my project team and the management class:  

- Work and knowledge are scattered across Slack, meetings, and documents  
- Strategies are rarely shared explicitly  
- Engagement improves mainly when there is advisor feedback  
- Members are often hesitant to share due to uncertainty or fear of low response  

These observations suggest that while the organization has strong individual contributors, it lacks a structured way to make collective knowledge visible and reusable.  

Through informal interactions (meetings, Slack communication, and observing participation patterns), I found that:  

- Awareness of others’ work slightly improves when visibility increases  
- Engagement increases when clear examples and structure are provided  
- Participation drops when systems feel like extra overhead  

This initiative directly addresses these gaps by making collaboration more visible, accessible, and low-effort, supporting HAAG’s broader goal of improving coordination across distributed research teams.  

---

## 3. Procedures

The initiative introduces a set of lightweight, reproducible procedures designed to integrate into existing workflows.  

### Procedure Overview

### 1. Create Dedicated Sharing Spaces  
Set up Slack channels at different levels:  
- project team  
- management class  
- HAAG-wide  

**Purpose:** Centralize strategy sharing without disrupting existing communication.  

---

### 2. Introduce a Simple Posting Template  
Each post follows a minimal structure (2–3 lines):  
- **Insight:** tool, workflow, or strategy  
- **Blocker:** question or issue  

**Purpose:** Reduce effort while keeping posts high-value and easy to read.  

---

### 3. Define Engagement Model  
- React if useful  
- Optional replies  
- Encourage peer-to-peer interaction  

**Purpose:** Lower pressure and avoid overloading users.  

---

### 4. Set Posting Expectations  
- Encouraged weekly, but flexible  
- Target: ~1 post every 2–3 weeks per person  

**Purpose:** Maintain consistency without creating burden.  

---

### 5. Track and Evaluate Participation  

**Metrics:**  
- Number of posts  
- Reactions and replies  
- Types of contributions  

**Observe:**  
- Whether strategies are reused  
- Whether blockers receive responses  

**Purpose:** Assess effectiveness and guide iteration.  

---

### 6. Iterate and Highlight Valuable Contributions  
- Periodically review posts  
- Highlight useful strategies  
- Refine template based on participation and feedback  

**Purpose:** Reinforce value and improve adoption over time.  

---

These procedures are complete, low-effort, and reproducible, and they rely only on existing tools (Slack), making them realistic for adoption. Unlike formal reporting systems, this initiative focuses on small, high-signal contributions that are easier to produce and consume.  

---

## 4. Purpose of the Procedures

The purpose of these procedures is to:  

- Make process and strategy visible, not just products and outcomes  
- Enable knowledge reuse across individuals and teams  
- Provide a low-friction way to ask for help and share insights  
- Improve awareness, alignment, and a sense of collaborative community without adding heavy structure  

---

## 5. Next Steps and Integration

The next step is to pilot the procedures in selected teams and collect data and feedback.  

### Foreseen Challenges
- Participation may remain inconsistent  
- People may default to existing habits (main Slack channels, meetings)  
- Some may hesitate to share unfinished work or processes  
- The system may feel like additional effort without immediate payoff  

### Integration and Sustainability
- Early-stage support from team leads or advisors is important  
- Over time, the system is designed to become peer-driven  

Several design choices support long-term adoption:  
- Low effort → reduces friction  
- Fits existing workflows → no major behavior change required  
- Scalable across teams → applicable at project, class, and HAAG levels  
- Iterative design → adapts based on real usage  

A key insight is that collaboration systems are most effective when feedback loops are present—especially early on. Without feedback, participation drops quickly. Therefore, maintaining an active feedback loop is critical for sustainability.  

---

## 6. Future Work

Future development focuses on extending the initiative into a more structured knowledge system:  

- Build a shared strategy library from collected posts  
- Categorize insights by topic for easier reuse  
- Potentially use AI-assisted organization to extract patterns  
- Expand the pilot to more teams and refine based on usage  

This would transform the system from a strategy-sharing space into a structured, long-term knowledge repository.
