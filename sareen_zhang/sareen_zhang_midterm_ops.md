# Operations Questions

## Define what reoccurring tasks you are doing within your project group

- sending reminders for weekly reports and updates
- organizing and attending meetings
- tracking tasks and progress
- following up with team members
- communicating with stakeholders


## How are you assessing your role as the project manager and its effect on the overall project

I assess my role based on whether I am improving visibility and clarity without adding unnecessary overhead.

So far, I have been more focused on:
- observing team behavior
- introducing lightweight structure

This approach helps maintain flexibility, but it also means adoption of new processes is slower.


## What challenges & success have you observed in your group

**Challenges:**
- information is scattered
- communication is inconsistent
- engagement varies across members and is generally dropping

**Successes:**
- team members are capable and independent
- work progresses even without strict structure


## How are you collaborating with stakeholders to ensure your role is supporting and motivating the group

I mainly collaborate through:
- Slack communication
- meetings
- reminders and follow-ups

I try to introduce structure without forcing it, and focus on supporting the team rather than controlling how they work.


## What feedback have you received on your role (solicited or unsolicited)

Feedback has mostly been indirect:
- people respond to reminders
- some engagement increases when structure is clearer

I have not received much direct feedback yet, which suggests I need to be more proactive in asking for it.


## What plans do you have for enhancing your role as the project manager with the remaining time in the semester

- simplify the initiative further to reduce effort required
- actively collect feedback from team members
- provide clearer examples and templates
- better integrate the system into existing workflows


## Please provide feedback on what support you may need in your role

- examples of effective collaboration systems in similar teams
- guidance on balancing structure vs flexibility
- feedback on whether this initiative direction is effective
