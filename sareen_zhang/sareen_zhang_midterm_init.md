# Initiative Questions

## Describe your initiative / Procedure

My initiative focuses on creating a shared space where team members can both show what they are working on and how they are doing it.

Right now, most work is shared through Slack or meetings. This makes it hard to see the actual process behind the work, and useful strategies are rarely shared across the team.

To address this, I would like to introduce a lightweight structure where people can:
- share weekly updates (what I did + how I approached it)
- post quick strategies (tools, workflows, lessons learned)

The goal is to make process and thinking visible, not just outcomes, so people can learn from each other more naturally.


## Explain the hypotheses / KPIs you have measured and what is left to be measured

**Hypotheses:**
- If strategies are visible, people will reuse ideas instead of working in isolation
- If work is visible across the team, awareness and alignment will improve
- Keeping the system lightweight will increase participation

**What I have observed so far:**
- Awareness of others’ work has slightly improved during meetings
- Engagement increases when examples are provided

**What still needs to be measured:**

A platform needs to be built. This can be a Slack channel (or Canvas), a GitHub repo, or a separate forum (maybe Ed). Within the platform, structure needs to be identified to organize different topics, posting format needs to be introduced, and an interaction diagram needs to be drawn. A system should be created to track posts, responses, applications, and whether strategies can be extracted and recorded.

Some metrics are:
- Participation rate (how many people actually post)
- Whether shared strategies are reused
- Perceived usefulness of the system
- Changes in cross-team awareness


## Explain your method for testing these hypotheses

The testing process is iterative:

- Observe current behavior (low visibility, scattered information)
- Introduce a simple sharing structure (first prototype, lightweight)
- Provide examples to guide participation
- Track participation and engagement
- Collect informal feedback
- Adjust based on what people actually use

**Flow:**
Work → Share (what + how) → Others view/use → Feedback → Iterate


## Explain how stakeholders are engaging with your initiative

Engagement has been lower than expected so far.

Most team members:
- continue using Slack and meetings as their main way of sharing
- do not consistently document their strategies or process

This shows that:
- the system may feel like extra work
- the value is not immediately clear

**Adjustments I am making:**
- reducing the effort required (shorter, more informal posts)
- focusing more on quick “strategy drops” instead of structured updates
- providing clearer examples
- drafting strategies to increase incentive and motivation (leaderboard, extra credit, community building, etc.)


## What processes have you documented or begun documenting

So far, I have started documenting:
- a simple format for sharing (what + how)
- examples of useful strategy posts

These will be organized in a shared space (GitHub / workspace still being finalized).

**Planned documentation:**
- lightweight templates
- clear guidelines for posting
- examples of effective contributions

The goal is to keep the system easy to follow and sustainable.


## How are you currently measuring progress toward your goals

Progress is currently measured through:
- participation (who is sharing)
- visibility (whether people are aware of others’ work)
- engagement (responses, discussions)

**Current signals:**
- slight improvement in awareness
- inconsistent participation


## What obstacles or bottlenecks have you encountered

The main challenges are:
- low participation
- people defaulting to existing habits
- unclear expectations for sharing

An unexpected issue is that some people hesitate to share unfinished work or their process. It is also not clear how much overhead this will create for participants and whether the benefits will outweigh the extra work.
