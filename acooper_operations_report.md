## Define what reoccurring tasks you are doing within your project group?

I am primarily focuses on task tracking. The Charleston train group is still in the exploratory phase, which means that structure and deliverables are not super clear yet. Instead, the lack of structure, and the ambiguity of the tasks means that tracking progress towards the (ambiguous) goals is not well defined. This makes it harder for researchers to know how the overall project is doing on a scope longer than one to two weeks. My focus on task tracking
is aimed at remedying this.

For that, I have been encouraging researchers to submit their weekly reports, and memorializing what they report. Their reports are not always super clear, as tasks can subtly change over the course of just one week (due to the exploratory nature of the project.) I have attempted to publicize the tracked tasks over time, but this has been a work in progress.

## How are you assessing your role as the project manager and its effect on the overall project. 

When I joined the project, researchers were not submitting any weekly reports, and told me when I interviewed them that they didn't have a clear long term view of the progress and successes of the group. There has been a marked changed in this — now researchers consistently submit their weekly reports. In addition, tasks have become more concrete, actionable and it has become easier to know if they are completed.
