## Initiative/Procedure

My HAAG Collaboration Procedure is still evolving, but right now it's a structured system that standardizes how teams collaborate by ensuring everyone stays visible, responsive, and engaged with one another. It runs on weekly reports, Slack communication, and adaptive collaboration practices that shift based on what the team needs. The goal is to keep everyone aware of what others are working on, actively giving feedback, and communicating progress and blockers, no matter how the team is organized.

---

## Explain the hypotheses/KPIs you have measured at this time and what is left to be measured.

The procedure tracks collaboration effectiveness through submission KPIs: consistent weekly report submission, completion of peer reviews, visible Slack communication, early identification of blockers, and demonstrated awareness of team members' work. These show us whether collaboration is actually happening and whether people are being transparent. I haven't yet measured long-term outcomes like project velocity or quality improvements tied directly to these practices.

## Explain how stakeholders are engaging with your initiative.

Managers and team members are expected to submit weekly reports, review peers' work, participate in meetings, and stay active on Slack. The procedure is designed to make engagement consistent and visible across the whole team. Right now, adoption has dropped off, and I need to reintroduce the procedure to the team, this time with clearer benefits so people understand why it matters.

## How are you currently measuring progress toward your goals.

I track observable behaviors: regular report submission, active peer feedback, consistent Slack communication, and early surfacing of blockers. Success looks like strong team-wide awareness and continuous feedback. Challenges show up as missing reports, lack of engagement, or poor communication. Right now, I'm seeing low adoption after the initial introduction, so the main challenge is getting people to actually start using the system.

---

## What obstacles or bottlenecks have you encountered

The anticipated challenge of maintaining engagement has materialized. People tried it briefly but didn't stick with it. The unexpected issue is that I didn't frontload the "why" enough. I need to reintroduce the procedure with clearer benefits and possibly simplify the initial commitment so it's easier to start.
