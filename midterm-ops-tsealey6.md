## Define what recurring tasks you are doing within your project group.

I oversee the weekly report collection and review cycle, participate in team meetings to identify blockers and shared deliverables, monitor Slack channels for visibility gaps, and follow up with individuals who miss checkpoints. I also track our four collaboration indicators (reports submitted, peer reviews completed, Slack activity visible, and team-wide awareness being confirmed) and adjust our collaboration mode based on what the team needs that week.

---

## How are you assessing your role as the project manager and its effect on the overall project?

I'm looking at whether the procedures I put in place actually reduce friction or just add overhead. If people are submitting reports but not reading each other's work, that's a signal that my role isn't creating value. I'm also watching whether issues get resolved faster with me tracking them versus potentially before I stepped in.

---

## What challenges and success have you observed in your group?

**Challenge:** Team members weren't aware of the weekly report requirement and essentially fought against it.

**Success:** When people submit their weekly reports and stay active on Slack, engagement stays consistent, and things stay on track. The peer reviews catch real issues early, anyone can step in to address them, and the weekly meeting gives us space to talk through what matters.

---

## How are you collaborating with stakeholders to ensure your role is supporting and motivating the group?

I'm checking in individually with team members to understand what's blocking them from participating, not just demanding compliance. I'm also asking what format would be useful to them, rather than assuming my way works for everyone.

A teammate also asked me directly what my purpose was and whether I'd just add more work to their plate, which pushed me to clarify I'm here to reduce friction, not create it.
